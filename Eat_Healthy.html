  • Seeds and Nuts Laddoos:

    For all the sweet tooth fitness freaks out there, presenting Oil Free, Sugar Free delectable "Seeds and Nuts Laddoo".

    Ingredients :

    • Almonds- One Handful (20-25)
    • Peanuts- One Handful (30-35)
    • Seed mix (readily available - Chia seeds, watermelon seeds, flax seeds, pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, white sesame seeds)- 1 small bowl
    • Dessicated coconut- 1 tbsp
    • Dates-12-15
    • Raisins- 25-30
    • Anjeer- 12-15 (optional)
    • Corn flakes- 1tbsp
    • Oats- 1 tbsp
    • Green cardamom pods- 5-6
    • Saunf -1 tsp
    • Cinnamon- 1/2 tsp
    • Jaggery Powder (Gud)- 1 tbsp
    • Water- 1/2 cup
    • Pinch of salt

    Notes :

    1. You can use any seeds and nuts as per your choice/availability.
    2. You can adjust the level of sweetness as per your liking.


    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Soak dates, raisins and anjeer in half cup hot water for 10 minutes. Finely chop and keep aside in a large bowl. Save the water.
    2. Dry roast all the nuts and seeds on stove top for 3-5 minutes or Microwave for- Almonds- 1 min Peanuts- 1 min Dessicated coconut- 1 min Seed mix - 2 min Oats- 2 min Corn flakes- 2 min.
    3. Coarsely grind the almonds, peanuts, oats and corn flakes after removing peanut skin. Add in the large bowl.
    4. Add the roasted seed mix in the bowl.
    5. Coarsely grind cardamom and saunf. Add it to the bowl alongwith salt and cinnamon.
    6. In a pan take 1 tbsp jaggery and add the water used for soaking dates, raisins and anjeer. Allow it to boil on medium flames. Once the syrup thickens, switch off the flames. (5-10 minutes)
    7. Pour the jaggery syrup in the bowl. Mix well.
    8. Laddoo mixture is ready. Use your palms to bind small round laddoos. This mixture will yield around 15 small laddoos. Store in an airtight container.
    Enjoy!!!

  • Oats and Bananas Pancakes :

    Presenting Deliciously Fluffy and Healthy "Oats Banana Pancakes" made easily in your blender!
    Contains Egg!

    Ingredients :

    • Oats- 1 cup
    • Milk- 1 cup
    • Ripe Banana- 2 chopped
    • Eggs- 2 (Can use 1 small bowl curd too)
    • Cinnamon- Â½ tsp
    • Salt- Â¼ tsp
    • Baking Powder- 2 tsp
    • Jaggery- 1 tsp
    • Coconut/Olive oil- 2 tbsp
    • Vanilla essence (optional)

    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Dry roast oats for a few minutes. Let it cool and grind to a fine powder.
    2. To this powder, add all the other ingredients (except oil) and blend until smooth.
    3. Check the consistency of batter. The batter should not be too thick or too thin. Add more milk, if needed to adjust the consistency.
    4. Let the batter rest for 10 minutes. Add 1 tsp of oil and mix well.
    5. Heat a non-stick pan, grease the surface with little bit of oil. Add one small griddle of the pancake batter and cover it with a plate. (You can make 3-4 pancakes at once)
    6. Cook the pancakes for 3-4 minutes on medium low heat. Check the cooking side, it should be brown in colour and bubbles should form on top. Flip the pancake and cook till the edges are golden.
    7. Serve fresh pancakes with honey/maple syrup/chocolate syrup and fresh fruits/berries/nuts of your choice.
    Enjoy!!!

    • Cinnamon powder- Â½ tsp
    • Salt- pinch

  • Post-workout nutrient rich Shake :

    Presenting a tasty, nutrient-dense post workout "Red Delight", after that strenuous session for speedy muscle recovery. It is packed with Vitamin A, C, K, beta carotene, anti oxidants, iron, magnesium and folate among other micronutrients.

    Ingredients:

    • Beetroot (roughly chopped)- 1
    • Carrot (roughly chopped)- 1
    • Dates (chopped)- 4-5
    • Anjeer- 3-4 (optional)
    • Green cardamom- 3-4 pods (without outer skin)
    • Black pepper (Freshly ground)- 8-10
    • Cinnamon- 1 inch or Â½ tsp powder
    • Jaggery- 1 tsp (adjust as per your sweetness likings)
    • Salt- pinch
    • Milk- 1.5 cups ( around 350 ml)
    • Water- 100 ml for boiling

    Note :

    If you don't have dates, you can substitute it with some sweet fruits like chikoo and banana or dried dates (chuara).


    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Heat a deep bottom pan on high flame. Add water, beetroot, carrots and cinnamon stick.
    2. Once it boils, switch the flame to low. Cover and cook for 5-10 minutes, till carrots become soft. Turn off the gas.
    3. Add dates in the pan and cover for 5 minutes. Allow the mixture to cool.
    4. Once the mix cools, add it to a blender with the rest of the ingredients. Blend to a smooth consistency.
    5. You can add more milk to adjust the consistency. Tastes best when chilled.
    Top it with seeds or nuts or your choice.

  • Paneer Masala :

    Presenting Paneer Butter Masala to satiate those taste buds guilt-free.

    Ingredients :

    • Paneer cubes - 200gms
    • Onions (roughly chopped) - 3 medium
    • Tomato (roughly chopped) - 5 medium
    • Red Chilli Powder - 1/2tsp
    • Turmeric powder - 1/4tsb
    • Garam Masala - 1/2tsp
    • Coriander(Dhania) powder - 2tsp
    • Cumin(Jeera) powder - 1/2tsp
    • Salt - as per taste
    • Cashews - 10-12
    • Watermelon seeds - 1tbsp
    • Bay leaves - 2
    • Cinnamon stick(Dal chini) - 2
    • Green Cardamom - 2
    • Cloves - 4
    • Garlic - 8-10
    • Ginger - 1-2 inches
    • Green chillies (chopped) - 2-4
    • Capsicum (finely chopped) - 1 medium
    • Fresh Cream - 2tsp
    • Curd - 1tbsp
    • Kasuri Methi - 1tsp
    • Olive oil - 2tsp
    • Water - 1.5 cup (about 350ml)

    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Heat 1 tsp oil in a pan on medium flame. Once the oil becomes hot, add onion. Saute for 5-6 minutes till onions become light brown.
    2. Add tomato and cook covered for 6-8 minutes, till tomato become mushy.
    3. Now, add salt, red chilli, garam masala, cumin and coriander powder. Mix well and cook for another 5-6 minutes.
    4. Add cashews and watermelon seeds. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Allow the mix to cool.
    5. Once it cools, blend it with some water (about 50 ml) to a smooth consistency.
    6. In a mortar pestle, add ginger, garlic, green chillies and cloves to prepare a paste.
    7. In the same pan, add the remaining oil. Once oil becomes hot, add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom and bay leaves and fry for 1 minute on low flame.
    8. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for 30-40 seconds, till raw aroma goes away.
    9. Add capsicum and saute for 5-6 minutes on medium heat. Now, add the onion- garlic puree and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    10. Add freshly beaten cream and cook till oil leaves the side of the masala. Turn off the gas. Add curd and mix well.
    11. Turn on the flames to medium and cook again till you see oil leaving the side of the masala.
    12. Add, hot water (about 300ml) and let the curry boil. Once it boils, add paneer cubes. Cook for 3-4 minutes.
    13. Add kasuri methi and turn off the gas. Add a pinch of garam masala. Garnish with cream/curd and kasuri methi/coriander.
    14. Serve with jeera rice/roti/paratha. Tastes best when served hot. I served it with beetroot carrot thepla.(see the recipe below)
    Enjoy!!!

  • Beetroot and Carrot Thepla :

    Presenting Beetroot and Carrot Thepla. A tasty way to incorporate veggies in your meal.

    Cooking time: 40-45 minutes
    Calories count: approx. 150 per thepla

    Ingredients :

    • Beetroot(grated) - 1 medium
    • Carrot(grated) - 2 medium
    • Onion(grated) - 1 medium
    • Ginger(grated) - 1 inch (optional)
    • Green chillies (finely chopped) - 2
    • Ajwain - 1/2 tsp
    • Kasuri Methi - 1 tsp
    • Cumin powder - 1/2 tsp
    • Garam Masala - 1/2 tsp
    • Salt - as per taste
    • Ghee - 1 tsp
    • Multigrain flour - 1 cup
    • Sooji - 1/4 cup

    Note :

    You can also add millet flour, oats flour and gram flour(besan).


    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Combine all the ingredients in a large bowl and mix well.
    2. Start kneading the dough. Knead the dough well, cover and let it rest for half an hour.
    3. Make round balls, dust with flour and start rolling rotis. Heat a tawa at medium high flames and cook partly on both the sides.
    4. Apply ghee, flip and cook till golden spots start to appear. Repeat for the other side.
    5. Serve hot with dahi/raita/dal/bhaji.

  • Banana Cream Parfait :

    Presenting Banana Cream Parfait. Time to twist that old school banana shake nostalgia.

    Ingredients :

    • Ripe Bananas (brown skin) - 3
    • Milk - 100ml
    • Peanut Butter - 2tbsp
    • Salt - pinch
    • Orange juice - 200ml
    • Lemon juice - 1tbsp
    • Honey - 1tbsp
    • Cinnamon - 1/2tbsp
    • Fruits and nuts of your choice

    Click to checkout/hide the RECIPE!

    Recipe :

    1. Peel and cut bananas in about 1 inch pieces. Place in an airtight container and freeze overnight.
    2. Blend together frozen bananas, milk, peanut butter and salt to a smooth creamy consistency.
    3. Place the cream in the airtight container and freeze for a few hours.
    4. In a small saucepan, mix orange juice, lemon juice, honey and cinnamon.
    5. Place the pan on medium flames and cook for 5-6 minutes, till the juice reaches syrup like consistency.
    6. Chop fruits and nuts of your choice. To make the Parfait, alternate layers of fruits, cream and orange syrup. Top with nuts and orange syrup.
